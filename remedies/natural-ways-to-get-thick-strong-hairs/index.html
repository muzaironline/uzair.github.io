Natural Ways to Get Thick & Strong Hairs

by adminJune 6, 2020June 8, 2020
Spread the love

Although there is no way to change the structure of hair follicles, people can do much to make their hair appear thicker and reduce cracks and hair loss.

In this article, we list several natural treatments that a person can do at home to make their hair look and feel thicker.

Overview

Hair is a broad condition that is thin or thin. Men often suffer from male pattern baldness, but women and men may experience thin hair in their lifetime.

Thin hair can cause:

Natural aging
Genetics
Poor nutrition
Overuse of commercial hair products
Reactions or allergies to chemicals or substances
Disease
Whatever the reason, using daily products can make your hair look naturally thick.

These methods should not treat underlying conditions that can lead to thin hair, such as malnutrition or alopecia.

Regardless of whether additional treatment is needed, the following natural treatments can help you thin hair.

Remedies

There are natural ways to tie hair, but the results are different for each person.

Daily products that make your hair thicker include:

1. Eggs

Eggs contain high levels of protein, which is necessary for the body to build strong and thick hair. When used regularly, egg treatment can help to strengthen and strengthen it.

To use an egg treatment:

Beat together one or two eggs
Apply this on your scalp and damp hair
Keep the eggs on the scalp for 30 minutes
Wash your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water and mild shampoo
Alternatively, mix the eggs, oil and water. To use this method:

Stir in egg yolks, 1 tbsp olive oil and 2 tbsp of water
Apply this mixture on your scalp and dry hair
Leave for 15 minutes
Rinse with warm water and mild shampoo
Egg treatment can help strengthen your hair once or twice a week.

2. Olive oil

Olive oil contains plenty of omega-3 acids and other nutrients that are essential for overall health, including hair loss. When applied directly to the scalp and hair, olive oil can help promote thick hair.

Olive oil has the added benefit of smoothing your hair and relieving scalp.

To use olive oil:

Heat the oil to body temperature
Massage the hot oil into the scalp and hair
Keep it on your hair for 30 to 45 minutes
Wash the olive oil with a mild shampoo
Some people mix honey with olive oil, while others suggest using olive oil overnight with a shower cap.

3. Proper nutrition

A nutritious diet rich in healthy fats, protein and vitamins can help make your hair thinner or thinner. In fact, thin hair is a sign that a person is not getting enough nutrients.

To remedy this, people with thin hair should include the following nutrient-rich foods in their diet:

Salmon, protein and fatty acids are high
Eggs containing protein, omega-3s and iron
Walnuts, almonds and other nuts are a source of fatty acids
Greek yogurt, which is a source of protein
Green, black, pinto and other beans contain protein
A person should add 1 or 2 servings to any of the above foods in their daily diet. Adding 3 or 4 servings per week can also improve your hair health.

4. Orange puree

Vitamin C, pectin and acid in oranges can help a person’s hair in certain ways.

Vitamins and nutrients enhance the natural luster of your hair, making your hair look thicker.

The acid in orange helps to separate residues from hair products. These debris can impede hair growth. Unlike some other treatments, orange milk also tastes beautiful, which makes the treatment more enjoyable.

A person can combine orange juice with fresh orange as a hair treatment and massage the scalp and scalp. Put on your hair for 1 hour before rinsing with milk.

Some people prefer to use a light conditioner to re-hydrate their hair after an orange puree treatment.

5. Aloe gel

Many believe that aloe helps the skin, the skin and the hair. Applying aloe oil directly on the scalp and scalp will strengthen your hair and tighten it over time.

Many commercial products containing aloe as an active ingredient are readily available. These products are gels and creams that are often applied directly to the scalp and hair.

For homemade remedies, a person can apply a clean aloe gel on the scalp and let it sit for 30 minutes before rinsing. Mix some aloe coconut oil or olive oil.

People can use this treatment once or twice a week.

6. Avocado

Avocado is rich in vitamin E, which many believe is a good moisturizer. Make a simple avocado and rub it twice a week.

Rub Avocado:

Mix 1 tsp olive oil with 1 avocado
Apply this mixture on your hair and scalp
Let sit for about 30 minutes
Wash thoroughly with mild shampoo

7. Castor Oil

Castor oil is also rich in vitamin E and fatty acids. Vitamin E helps hair health.

It is easy to apply on your hair without making castor oil. Apply oil on your scalp and hair until the hair is coated. Keep the oil on your head for 30 minutes before rinsing.
