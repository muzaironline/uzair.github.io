Many people do not bother to read the information written on the label and think that they already know how to know about certain products. But sometimes, they are so wrong.

Bright Side has collected some surprising facts about products that we all eat almost every day.

Pringles aren’t made from potatoes.

© pixabay.com   © pixabay.com  

These chips are made with a special kind of flour. This includes wheat, rice, corn flour and dehydrated potato flakes. These are thin dough pieces fried, dried, and with powdered spices and other flavor enhancing sprays.

Almonds, peanuts, and pistachios aren’t nuts.

© pixabay.com   © pixabay.com  

From the botanical point of view, a nut is a “fruit” inside a hard fruit that does not open under natural conditions. “True” nuts are hazelnuts, chestnuts and acorns. An almond is a seed and a peanut is related to beans. Pistachios, walnuts and pine nuts are stones or seeds.

Carrots may have different colors.

© unsplash.com  

People started breeding orange carrots only in the 17th century. Earlier, there were purple, white and red carrots.

100% natural juice can’t be packaged.

© foodiesfeed.com  

To keep the juice fresh for a long time, the fruits are artificially deprived of oxygen. Unfortunately, they lose their taste and smell during this process. This is why manufacturers add aromatizers and other food additives. Because of this, we cannot say that the juice is 100% natural.

Oat flakes aren’t a 100% natural product.

© pixabay.com  

Many people think that oat flakes are not healthy. In fact, chemicals are sometimes used to dry oats faster. In 2010, in the USA, a lawsuit was filed against a well-known company that produces oat flakes. It contained ingredients hazardous to the health of people like trans fats. The company was accused of false advertising.

Maple syrup isn’t always healthy.

© pixabay.com  

Many people know that maple syrup is good for our health. It is considered a healthy alternative to sugar. But an inexpensive bottle of syrup may contain preservatives and artificial flavors.

It’s difficult to find real olive oil in modern shops.

© pixabay.com  

Olive oil is really healthy and useful. But if you want to buy it then you have to be careful. According to the National Consumers League, 2/3 of all bottles in supermarkets contain soybean oil with an olive flavor.

Caffeine-free coffee still contains caffeine.

© pixabay.com  

Talking about coffee, it has many advantages and disadvantages. If your doctor advises you to expel all substances that stimulate the nervous system, then you need to remember that each caffeine-free coffee pack contains 0.01 to 3% caffeine.

Fitness bars aren’t healthy.

© pixabay.com  

The popular muesli bar contains a large amount of sugar, salt, rapeseed oil and corn flour – it is not as healthy. What’s more, various chemical substances are used to dry the flakes. That is why in 2014 and 2016, a United States court forbade the Nature Valley company to use the “100% natural” label.

Marshmallows aren’t for vegetarians.

© pixabay.com  

Soft marshmallow contains gelatin which is made from animal skin and bones. The components are boiled and then starch and sweeteners are added. Before eating a product made of gelatin, remember what it is made of.

Bonus: Even bananas can hide dangerous surprises.

© www.9596.org  

Abby Woodgate, a woman from Britain, bought a bunch of bananas and when she came home she noticed a strange substance on the fruit. He thought it was just some mold and he tried to take it off so that he could use the rest of the bunches. Once Abby found out that the mold was a cocoon with eggs, she called the pest control experts.

© unknown user/ imgur  

Spider experts believe that the eggs were the spider eggs (the world’s deadliest spider) that roamed Brazil. The abbey was told by experts that it would have to burn anything that came in contact with the egg, because orchids are highly toxic.
