Technological advances have given us many opportunities, but now much work has been done with the help of computers, which means we spend hours on monitors.

Bright Side has prepared a bunch of exercises you can do anywhere for tired eyes.

1. Near and far focus

Do this exercise while standing or standing.

  • Keep your thumb in front of you for 10 seconds and focus on it for 10-15 seconds.
  • Focus on the object 10 to 20 feet away from you for 10-15 seconds.
  • Finally, focus on an object that is away from you (for example a tree or building) for another 10-15 seconds.

2. Side-to-side eye movement

Do this exercise while standing or standing.

  • Turn your head to the left and focus on the object you can see. Then look to the right and focus on the object you can see.
  • Move your eyes 5 times. Do this 3 times.

3. Up and down eye movement

© Depositphotos.com  

Do this exercise while standing or standing.

  • Look up and down and focus on what you see.
  • Move your eyes up and down 5 times in each direction. Do this 3 times.

4. Diagonal eye movement

© Depositphotos.com  

Do this exercise while standing or standing.

  • Look to the left. Move your eyes diagonally and look to the right. Focus on what you see.
  • Change directions. Look to the right and move your eyes to the left.
  • Exercise 5 times for each direction. Do this 3 times.

5. Circle eye movement

© Depositphotos.com  

Do this exercise while standing or standing.

  • Start rolling your eyes slowly clockwise. Draw 5 circles.
  • Change direction and roll your eyes in a counterclockwise circle. Draw 5 circles.
  • Do this 3 times.

6. Blinking

© Depositphotos.com  

Do this exercise while standing or standing.

  • Lighten 10-15 times per minute.
  • Do this exercise for 2 minutes.

7. Palming

© Depositphotos.com  

Pamming exercise is a great way to soothe the optic nerve.

  • In a dark room, rest your elbows on the table.
  • Put your palms on top of your eyes and rub your hands together.
  • Visualize the total blackness and inhale deeply. Exercise for 5 minutes.

8. The 20/20/20 exercise

© Depositphotos.com   © Depositphotos.com  

If you spend all day in front of a computer screen, give yourself a break every 20 minutes and look at 20 feet for 20 seconds. This exercise gives your eyes regular rest during the work day.

Bonus: Spa for the eyes

© Depositphotos.com   © Depositphotos.com   © Depositphotos.com  

Exercise is a great way to calm your eyes. If you want to increase the positive effect of these exercises, try masking your eyes. For example, there are only 3 ingredients in this guise:

  • 1 tbsp grated carrots
  • 1 egg white
  • Aloe 1 tbsp
  • Mix all ingredients in a bowl, coat the mixture around your eyes and leave the mask on for 15-30 minutes. Wash the mask with water.

© Depositphotos.com  

These exercises have proven themselves effective in relieving tired eyes. However, if you experience chronic eye problems or notice that your vision is getting worse, you may need to consult a doctor.
